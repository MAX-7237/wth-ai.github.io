Research Experience

TOPS: Efficient Visual Token Pruning for LVLM Inference

Designed a training-free visual token pruning framework with coarse token selection after the vision encoder and progressive refinement inside the LLM decoder. The work studies token pruning from a first-principles perspective and formulates it as a Token Optimal Preservation Set construction problem.

  • Identified three key principles for token selection: task relevance, information coverage, and semantic diversity.
  • Evaluated across multiple MLLM backbones and benchmarks under different token reduction ratios.
  • Demonstrated strong performance preservation under substantial visual token reduction.

PAFI: Training-Free Hallucination Mitigation in LVLMs

Conducted mechanistic analysis of hallucination in LVLMs and proposed a dual-stream intervention method that amplifies image-token attention while attenuating critical FFN outputs.

WildRoadBench

Contributed to a wild aerial road-damage grounding benchmark for vision-language models and autonomous agents, including evaluation protocol design and benchmarking of VLMs and LLM-driven agents.
